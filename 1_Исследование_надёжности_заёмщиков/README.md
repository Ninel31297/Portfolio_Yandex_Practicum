# **Project: Borrower Reliability Analysis — Banking Data Study**

## **Description**
**Data source:** Bank-provided dataset containing customer creditworthiness statistics  

**Objective:**  
Analyze how marital status and the number of children influence the likelihood of timely loan repayment.

## **Work Performed**
- Explored and validated the dataset  
- Identified and handled missing values  
- Corrected data types to ensure consistency  
- Removed duplicate records  
- Performed data categorization  
- Decomposed one dataframe into three for more structured analysis  

## **Tech Stack**
Pandas, Python, Data Preprocessing  

## **Key Findings**
- The presence of children affects the probability of loan default; however, additional data on large families is required for reliable conclusions  
- Customers who are or have been officially married demonstrate higher financial stability  
- Income level shows an inconclusive impact due to insufficient representation across income groups  

- **Lowest default risk categories:**
  - Real estate transactions — 7.25%  
  - Wedding-related expenses — 7.91%  

- **Highest default risk categories:**
  - Education — 9.25%  
  - Car-related expenses — 9.34%  

## **Conclusion**
Marital status and loan purpose are important features for credit scoring models.  
Additional data is required to draw robust conclusions regarding income level and number of children.
